Teaching and Mentoring Experience

Both of my parents are teachers. My father is a retired Math teacher & middle school principal, and my mother is a retired Linguistics professor. I inherited their teaching philosophy, believing that (1) each student is unique and needs to be treated as such, (2) encouragement gives students a lasting boost, and (3) teachers should raise the bar and work with students to see how high they can jump.

When mentoring junior scientists at work, I strive to be accessible, supportive, encouraging, equitable, and respectful. I strive to be open-minded, enjoy seeing my mentees become more independent, and look forward to us growing together.

While leading projects, I have also worked with some other research associates and associate scientists for assay development and troubleshooting.


9/2014–8/2021 | Teaching Assistant | Texas A&M University, College Station, TX

For 18 semesters, I was teaching the lab sections of courses including:

• Introductory Biology I,
• Introductory Biology II (taxonomy and evolution),
• Introductory Microbiology, and
• Integrated Human Anatomy and Physiology.

As a teaching assistant, I was:

• Preparing and presenting weekly pre-lab lectures.
• Preparing quiz and exam questions; grading quizzes, exams, and assignments (lab reports).
• Teaching students proper laboratory techniques.
• Assisting students in performing experiments.
• Being available to students outside of laboratory hours to provide resources and assistance.
• Maintaining a safe learning environment by ensuring adherence to safety protocols.
